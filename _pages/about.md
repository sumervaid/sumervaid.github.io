Welcome to my homepage. I am a computational psychologist (postdoctoral research associate) at Harvard Business School. Previously, I earned my Ph.D. and M.A. in media psychology at Stanford. Before that, I earned a B.A. and M.A in Psychology, and a graduate certificate in computational social science from the University of Chicago. In my research, I examine how creating a psychological fit between people, their environments and their behaviors results in healthy outcomes like increased wellbeing, persuasion and productivity. I study psychological fit in a variety of domains ranging from mobile and social media to organizations and markets. In my free time, I like to do high-intensity interval training and make customized nitro cold brews. Fun-fact: I dreamed of being a cosmologist before I read my first psychology paper.
